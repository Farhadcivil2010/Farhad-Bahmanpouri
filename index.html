Projects

  • UAWOS – Unmanned Airborne Water Observing System
    Collaborative project with top European institutions (e.g., TUM, DTU) and companies to establish UAS-based hydrometry as a standard for remote river monitoring. Key goals include reducing costs (by 2–3 times), enabling contactless discharge measurements (<15% error), enhancing hydraulic model input, and validating EO-based water level observations.
    Project Link
  • FLORIS – Flood Risk Forecasting and Hydraulic Vulnerability Assessment (2023–2025)
    Development of probabilistic flood forecasting systems and 2D hydraulic models to assess flood impacts, especially in areas with vulnerable embankments.
    • Enhanced forecasting and uncertainty quantification
    • Hydraulic hazard and floodplain delineation
  • FLOSET – Probabilistic Floods and Sediment Transport Forecasting (2022–2024)
    Indo-Italian collaboration to model velocity distribution, discharge, and bed load transport in Himalayan rivers using limited field data.
    • Hydrodynamic modeling and sediment transport estimation
    • Statistical analysis using Python
  • ENTERPRISING – Predicting River Flows and Biotic Impacts During High Floods
    Multidisciplinary project integrating radar, biosensors, and hydrodynamic models to monitor river flow and assess the effects on river ecosystems.
    • Development of no-contact flow monitoring technologies
    • Modeling hydro-biotic interactions
    • Field and lab testing of a Biological Early Warning System (BEWS)
  • Clim-Amazon – Brazilian-European Climate Research on the Amazon Basin
    Study of hydrodynamics, sediment transport, and stratigraphy at major river confluences in the Amazon to understand mixing and morphological processes.
  • Caspian Sea Wave Modelling (2012–2014)
    As a member of Namrood Co., contributed to wave and coastal modeling near Iran’s Caspian Sea coast using MIKE21 and PMODynamics models.
    • Wave and sediment transport modeling using satellite data
    • Monitoring of coastal conditions: sea level, currents, meteorology
  • Entropy-Based Flow and Sediment Analysis
    Application of the Entropy concept to estimate velocity distribution, discharge, and sediment transport in rivers with limited field data.
  • ADCP Data Processing and Visualization
    Development of tools and scripts to process and visualize ADCP (Acoustic Doppler Current Profiler) data for hydrodynamic analysis.
  • Flood Modeling in the Nestore River with HEC-RAS
    Hydraulic and flood modeling using HEC-RAS 6.6 for the Nestore River, a tributary of the Tiber River, including geometry setup and simulation of flood events.
